TAO (ACE ORB) Metrics

A decade of intense R&D has yielded ACE, which is an object-oriented framework that implements many core design patterns for concurrent communication software. We have applied the patterns and components in the ACE framework to develop The ACE ORB (TAO), which is our standards-based, CORBA middleware framework. TAO applies the best software practices and patterns that we have discovered in our work on ACE in order to automate the delivery of efficient, scalable, and predictable real-time quality-of-service (QoS) to distributed applications. Both ACE and TAO are freely available, open-source software that been funded by the DARPA Quorum program, NSF, and many visionary industrial sponsors.

The release of the first commercially supported (but still open-source) version of TAO is a momentous step. This release comes at a time when open-source software is achieving critical mass, and users are not just accepting it, but recognizing its place in the mix of software development models. In particular, a rapidly increasing number of projects and sponsors are applying TAO to help lower their development cost, improve their QoS, and decrease their time-to-market.
